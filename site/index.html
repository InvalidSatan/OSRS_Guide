Introduction

Welcome to a comprehensive Old School RuneScape (OSRS) members' guide for reaching level 99 in all skills and preparing for end-game bossing and item farming. This guide is written in a gentle, step-by-step manner, focusing on high experience rates and AFK-friendly methods. We'll also highlight essential quests that provide huge benefits or unlocks.

General Tips

  • Balance high experience rates with AFK-friendly methods like crabs for combat or Motherlode Mine for Mining.
  • Use quest rewards to skip slow early levels—Waterfall Quest alone grants 13,750 Attack and Strength XP.
  • Unlock helpful tools early, such as the graceful outfit or the herb sack, to speed up other skills.
  • Manage your money: some skills are expensive, so mix profitable training with costlier methods.
  • Complete achievement diaries to unlock useful teleports and perks.
  • When AFKing, use tools like RuneLite's idle notifier and wear appropriate gear to stay safe.

Combat Skills

Maxing your combat skills (Attack, Strength, Defence, Hitpoints, Ranged, Magic, Prayer, and Slayer) dramatically increases your effectiveness in boss fights. Complete quests such as Waterfall Quest, Tree Gnome Village, and Fight Arena early on to catapult your levels, then train alongside Slayer for steady progress.

  • Early quests like Waterfall, Tree Gnome Village and Fight Arena boost combat levels rapidly.
  • Train at Sand or Ammonite Crabs for relaxed XP up to the 70s.
  • Use Nightmare Zone or Slayer tasks for efficient high-level training.
  • Unlock Piety via King's Ransom and obtain a Dragon Defender early.

Melee Training

  • Quest to 30+ Attack and Strength, then camp Sand or Ammonite Crabs until the 70s.
  • Upgrade to a Dragon scimitar after Monkey Madness I and work toward a Dragon Defender.
  • Dharok's or absorption potions in Nightmare Zone allow extremely AFK training.

Ranged

  • Complete Animal Magnetism by level 30 for Ava's device.
  • Train with a cannon on Slayer tasks or safespot at Sand Crabs.
  • Use knives or darts for quick mid-level XP, then swap to broad bolts or chinchompas.

Magic

  • Cast strike spells or use quest rewards for early levels.
  • High Alchemy and teleport spells offer convenient XP while skilling.
  • Bursts and barrages on Slayer tasks give the fastest but costly XP rates.

Non-Combat Skills

For every non-combat skill, this guide emphasizes a balance of high XP rates and AFK approaches. Examples include using The Tourist Trap lamps to skip early Agility levels and gathering marks of grace on rooftop courses. Focus on unlocking teleports and quality-of-life items to make training less tedious.

  • Agility: use The Tourist Trap and The Grand Tree for early XP, then farm marks at Canifis rooftops for your Graceful set.
  • Thieving: complete Fight Arena and The Feud, then pickpocket Ardougne Knights or blackjack bandits.
  • Prayer: train with dragon bones on a gilded or Chaos altar for fast levels.
  • Crafting and other skills benefit from daily activities like battlestaves and kingdom management.
  • Construction: build oak larders to 74 Construction before switching to dungeon doors.
  • Herblore: use quest rewards to reach 38, then make prayer potions and super sets for profit.
  • Farming: plant herb runs daily and manage the kingdom for a steady income.
  • Hunter: unlock Fossil Island and do bird house runs for passive experience.
  • RuneCrafting: quest to level 50 and train at Guardians of the Rift for fast XP.
  • Fletching: craft broad arrows or darts while AFKing other activities.

Essential Quests

Quests provide huge experience rewards and vital unlocks. Waterfall Quest grants immediate combat levels, while quests like Recipe for Disaster and Desert Treasure unlock powerful gear and teleports. Prioritize quests that boost training efficiency and open up new content.

  • Early: Waterfall Quest, The Grand Tree, Fight Arena, and Tree Gnome Village.
  • Mid-game: Recipe for Disaster for Barrows Gloves, Fairy Tale II for fairy rings, and King's Ransom for Piety.
  • Late-game: Song of the Elves, Sins of the Father, and Desert Treasure II unlock end-game areas and bosses.

End-Game Preparation

With maxed skills and key quests complete, you'll be ready to farm bosses for valuable drops. High-level gear, prayers, and a well-rounded account will allow you to take on OSRS's toughest challenges.

  • Aim for Barrows gear and a Dragon Defender early, then upgrade to Bandos and other high-tier items.
  • Start learning bosses like Barrows, Giant Mole, and King Black Dragon for basic mechanics.
  • Progress to tougher encounters such as Zulrah and Vorkath before tackling raids.
  • Bring high-healing food and prayer potions to extend trips at early bosses.
  • Watch video guides to learn raid mechanics before attempting Chambers of Xeric or Theatre of Blood.

Boss Strategies

  • Barrows: use protect-from-Melee when entering tunnels and bring a spade plus magic switch gear.
  • Giant Mole: follow it with a light source and use overhead prayers for safety.
  • Zulrah: learn the rotation patterns and use range gear with quickswap magic attacks.
